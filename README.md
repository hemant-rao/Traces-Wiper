# Dig-deep-delete

A privacy-focused Android application designed to secure your internal storage.

## Purpose
Deleted files are rarely truly gone on modern Android devices due to flash storage wear leveling and encryption. Instead of providing misleading "recovery" features, Dig-deep-delete focuses on **honest privacy protection**:

- **Deep Wipe**: Overwrites free space on your internal storage with random data. This ensures that remnants of previously deleted files stored in free clusters are destroyed, making them unrecoverable by common recovery tools.

## Key Features
- **Secure Free Space Wipe**: A reliable utility to overwrite the device's free space with secure, randomized data.
- **Transparent Privacy**: We do not claim to recover previously deleted files. We inform users that on modern Android (FBE encryption + Flash storage), true secure deletion happens when free space is managed, or by factory resetting the device to clear the encryption key.

## Disclaimer
- Internal storage is encrypted and uses flash memory, so massive amounts of "deleted" data are often already gone.
- This free-space wipe is a best-effort extra measure, not a 100% guarantee.
- For a complete, guaranteed wipe (destroying the encryption key), perform a full factory reset.

## Technical Details
- **Tech Stack**: Kotlin, Jetpack Compose, Material 3.
- **Wiping Logic**:
  - **FreeSpaceWiper**: Overwrites available free space with secure random data generated by `SecureRandom`.
  - **SecureDeleter**: Performs a secure deletion pass on specific files before removing them, using `RandomAccessFile` with sync to ensure data is written to disk.
